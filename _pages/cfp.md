## Submission Formats

**Full papers:** Up to 8 pages (excluding references), using the COLM formatting guidelines.

**Short papers / Extended abstracts:** Up to 4 pages describing work in progress, case studies, or position statements.

Submissions will follow a **double-blind review process** and must be anonymized. Workshop papers will be **non-archival**, and authors will retain the right to submit extended versions to archival venues. We use [OpenReview](https://openreview.net) for submission handling.

We particularly encourage submissions from early-career researchers and practitioners, including works in progress, negative or null results, and analyses that may not yet fit the format of a full conference paper but raise important questions for the language modeling community.

---

## Topics of Interest

Across all contributions, we are especially interested in work that uses high-stakes deployment as a lens for examining assumptions in contemporary language modeling.

- AI and machine learning for decision-making in high-stakes domains
- Reliability, robustness, and safety under distribution shift and adversarial conditions
- Evaluation methodologies and benchmarks for high-stakes AI applications
- Explainability, interpretability, and transparency for consequential AI decisions
- Human-in-the-loop and decision support in critical settings
- Bias, fairness, and equity in AI systems affecting protected or vulnerable populations
- Responsible deployment and lifecycle management in regulated environments
- AI risk assessment, incident analysis, and failure modes in real-world deployments
- Regulatory and legal considerations for high-risk AI systems
- Use of large language models and foundation models in high-stakes applications
- Case studies, post-mortems, and lessons learned from deployed AI systems
- Cross-disciplinary perspectives bridging AI research with domain expertise
- Societal, ethical, and economic impacts of AI in critical decision-making contexts

---

## Review Process

Each submission will be reviewed by at least two reviewers drawn from a program committee consisting of researchers from academia and industry. Reviews will follow a double-blind process and emphasize **relevance, clarity, and potential impact** rather than maturity alone. Conflicts of interest will be handled in accordance with the COLM conflict-of-interest policy.
